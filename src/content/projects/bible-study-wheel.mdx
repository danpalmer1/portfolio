## What it is

A side project that escaped the side-project graveyard. **Bible Study
Wheel** is a small app for tracking a structured Bible reading rotation —
think habit-tracker meets reading plan. It runs at
[studywheel.app](https://studywheel.app) and has real users.

## Why I built it

I wanted to ship something I would actually use myself, on a stack I
hadn't touched at work, with a design language that didn't look like
every other dark dashboard.

The "Cool Woodland" aesthetic — mossy greens, lichen grays, warm cream
backgrounds, Fraunces display + Inter body — came out of asking *"what
would this look like if it felt physical, like a leather-bound journal
that happened to live on the web?"*

## What it taught me

- **Tailwind v4 in production.** Different mental model than v3 — the
  `@theme` block becomes the source of truth and most of my `tailwind.config`
  habits had to be unlearned.
- **Supabase row-level security is the whole ballgame.** If you get the
  policies right, the client can talk to the database directly. If you
  don't, you find out in a way you don't enjoy.
- **Real users surface what you can't.** I had three bugs in the first
  week that I'd never have found from my own usage. Two of them were
  embarrassing.

## What's next

A handful of features that emerged from actual use — saved progress
across devices, shareable wheels, a print-friendly view. Slow burn, not
on a deadline.
