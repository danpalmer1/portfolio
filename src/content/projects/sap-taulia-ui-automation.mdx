## The work that wasted everyone's time

Each week, an analyst on our team logged into SAP Taulia, walked through the
UI for each of ~20 customer accounts, exported a report, and stitched the
results into a single spreadsheet for downstream reporting. The workflow
was ~8 hours of mechanical clicking — every single week.

No public API. No batch export. Just a person and a browser.

## What I built

A UiPath bot that drives the SAP Taulia UI the same way a human would, but
without the human:

```python
# Sketch of the per-customer loop the bot runs each week.
# Real implementation is a UiPath workflow, but the logic mirrors this.

for customer in customers:
    page = sap.navigate("/taulia/customer-detail")
    page.fill("#customer-id", customer.id)
    page.click("button[data-action=submit]")
    page.wait_for("table.results")

    rows = page.scrape_table("table.results")
    spreadsheet.append_rows(rows, source=customer.id)

spreadsheet.save_as(f"weekly-taulia-{week_iso}.xlsx")
mail.send(to=analyst, attachments=[spreadsheet.path])
```

The bot runs unattended on a schedule, opens its own browser session,
handles login through our SSO, drops the finished spreadsheet in the same
place the analyst used to put it, and emails the team when it's done.

## Outcome

- **8 hours/week recovered** — the analyst got their morning back
- **~400 hours/year** across the team
- **Zero typos** — the bot doesn't fat-finger a customer ID
- **Same output format** — downstream consumers didn't need to change anything

## What I'd do differently

If I were building this today, I'd reach for an API contract first. SAP
Taulia has since opened more programmatic surfaces, and any UI-driven bot
is one DOM redesign away from a maintenance fire drill.

The lesson I took into AI work: **automate the workflow, not the UI**.
