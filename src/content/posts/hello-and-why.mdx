This site is the public version of a notebook I've kept privately for a
few years. The notebook is mostly two things: short notes on automations
I've built (and what surprised me about each one), and longer reflections
on what changes when you let a model do the part of the work that used to
require a person.

I'm putting it online for one reason: the conversations I've had about
this work have all been better than the ones I haven't. Writing publicly
is a way to invite more of them.

## What you'll find here

- **Project case studies** — the ones in `/work`. Not "look at my code,"
  but "look at the problem and the path." I care more about why a build
  was the right shape than how clever the code was.
- **Short posts** — things I notice. Patterns that repeat across
  automations. Things I got wrong. Pieces of advice I would have wanted
  three years ago.

## What you won't find here

- Tutorial content with affiliate links.
- Hot takes on whether AI is going to replace developers.
- Anything I wouldn't say to the same audience over coffee.

## Cadence

Slow on purpose — better to post one thing I actually have a point about
than five things I don't.
